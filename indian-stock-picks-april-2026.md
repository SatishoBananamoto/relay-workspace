## Dynamic Stop Loss Rules

As positions move in your favor, trail stops up:

**General rule:** When a stock rises 15%+ from entry, move SL to entry+2% (lock in breakeven). When it rises 25%+, move SL to entry+15%. At target, sell 50% and trail the rest with an 8% trailing stop.

- India imports 85% of oil — higher crude = wider current account deficit
- RBI has 620B+ USD forex reserves as buffer

**Impact on picks:**
- SBI: Medium risk (if oil inflation forces RBI hike, thesis weakens)
- Tata Motors: Medium-High (JLR margins squeezed by energy costs)

**Mitigation:** Tranched entry absorbs further dips. Stop losses protect capital. Oil at $115 is geopolitical-premium (Iran), not demand-driven — typically fades.

- FIIs pulled 52,704 Cr from Indian equities in March 2026
- Single-day outflow of 10,717 Cr (largest of 2026)
- DIIs absorbing the selling, providing floor
